Final Technical Guide: UI/UX Competition

This document is the official technical guide, updated according to the change to a team-based competition system. All participants are expected to follow this guide in preparing their submissions.

Download Full Booklet

A. Submission Requirements

Each team is required to submit the following three main components:

  1. High-Fidelity Figma Prototype Link (View-Only Access).
  2. Presentation Pitch Deck (.PPT or .PDF Format).
  3. Demonstration Video (YouTube/Google Drive Link).

B. Detailed Pitch Deck Guide (Max. 25 Slides)

The Pitch Deck is a primary assessment component that must narrate your team's entire process, from idea to solution.

1. Cover & Team Identity

  • Work Title & Team Name.
  • Team Identity: Include the full names of all members (2-3 people) along with their main roles/contributions in the project (e.g., UX Researcher, UI Designer, Project Manager).
  • The chosen sub-theme and SDG.

2. Research & Idea Validation

  • Background & Problem: Explain the context, urgency, and specific problem you aim to solve.
  • Research MethodMandatory: Min. 1

    To validate your problem, choose and conduct at least ONE of the following research methods:

    • User Interview: Conduct in-depth interviews with 3-5 people who are your target audience.
    • Online Survey: Distribute an online questionnaire to obtain quantitative data.
    • Competitor Analysis: Analyze 2-3 existing similar products/applications to find their strengths and weaknesses.
    • Secondary Research (Desk Research): Collect and analyze existing data, articles, journals, or reports to support your argument about the urgency of the problem.
    • Forum & Review Analysis: Dig for insights from reviews on the App Store/Play Store or discussions in online forums (like Reddit, etc.) to find real user complaints and needs.
  • Research Findings & Persona:

    Present the key findings from your research and summarize them in the form of:

    • User Persona: One representative fictional user profile.
    • Pain Points: 3-5 main difficulties faced by the persona.

3. Ideation & Solution Priority

  • Show how your team brainstormed to generate solution ideas.
  • Explain the main features you propose and provide reasons why these features are a priority.

4. User Flow

  • Illustrate the most crucial user interaction flow in using your solution, from start to goal achievement.

5. Design Showcase (High-Fidelity Screens)

  • Present several of the most representative screens from your application.
  • Also, include a simple Design System (Colors, Typography, Icons) that you used to maintain consistency.

6. Vision & Development Potential

Explain the long-term vision of your project. You can choose 1-2 of the areas below that are most relevant to describe:

  • Product Evolution: What's the next big feature? How will this product evolve in the next year?
  • Growth & Outreach: How will you reach more users or collaborate with other parties?
  • Sustainable Impact: How will you measure the success of your project's social impact?

7. Closing & Prototype Link

  • Close your presentation with a strong conclusion.
  • Re-include the view-only link to your interactive prototype in Figma.

C. Detailed Demonstration Video Guide

The demonstration video is a mandatory component aimed at showcasing the function and value of your solution visually and engagingly.

1. Duration & Format

  • Maximum duration: 3 minutes.
  • Minimum video resolution of 1080p (Full HD) with MP4 file format.

2. Audio & Language

  • Narration (voice-over) in English with clear audio quality is mandatory.
  • Videos without narration or with only music/text will not be judged maximally.

3. Video Structure & Content (Sequential Order Required)

  • Opening (0-20 seconds):
    • Introduce the project title, team name, and all members.
    • State the problem and the SDG being addressed.
    • Display the Portal 7 & Ditmawa logos.
  • Scenario-Based Demonstration (± 2 minutes):
    • Must show one most crucial user flow from start to finish.
    • Narrate the scenario being performed by the user, not just randomly clicking buttons.
  • Closing (± 30 seconds):
    • Provide a brief statement about the expected impact of your solution.
    • End the video with a call-to-action or hope for future development.

D. Appendix: Research Proof FormatRecommended

To strengthen your arguments and validation, it is highly recommended to include research proof as an appendix at the end of your presentation (within the 25-slide limit). Here are the suggested formats:

For User Interviews:

  • Documentation: Screenshot of the conversation (chat) or a photo of the interview session (interviewee's face may be blurred for privacy).
  • Summary: A simple table containing a list of key questions and a summary of the most important answers or quotes from the interviewees.

For Short Surveys:

  • Link & Demographics: Include a link to the active survey (e.g., Google Forms) and a summary of respondent demographics (e.g., "50 respondents, 60% students, 40% workers").
  • Key Results: Screenshot 2-3 of the most relevant charts or diagrams from your survey results that support your main arguments.

For Secondary Research:

  • List of Sources: Create a list of the sources you used (e.g., Article/Report Title, Author, Year, and Link). 3-5 of the most relevant sources are sufficient.

For Forum & Review Analysis:

  • Screenshot Collection: Present 3-5 of the most representative screenshots of reviews (from App Store/Play Store) or comments/discussions (from online forums). Highlight or mark the most important parts of the text.
